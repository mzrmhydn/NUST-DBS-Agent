================================================================================
NUST University Database - Schema Summary & Design Decisions
Scope: Undergraduate (Bachelor's) Programs (one 2-year MSIS entry for diversity)
DBMS: MySQL 8.0+ (InnoDB)
================================================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
The database models two tightly coupled modules that share the `Program` table:

  1. ACADEMIC MODULE
     School, Faculty, Program, Course, Prerequisite, ProgramCourse,
     Student, Term, Classroom, Section, Enrollment

  2. ADMISSIONS MODULE
     Applicant, EntryTest, TestAttempt, Application, Offer

The bridge between modules: when an applicant's Application is selected, an
Offer is issued and a new `Student` row is created. Unlike a strict
Application-bridge design, `Student` carries `ProgramID` and `ApplicantID`
directly so that the most common academic queries (transcript, class roster,
CGPA) do not require a join through the admissions pipeline.

KEY DESIGN DECISIONS
--------------------
1. All primary keys are surrogate IDs except for `Course` (CourseCode is the
   natural PK) and the junction tables Prerequisite, ProgramCourse, and
   TestAttempt (composite PKs).
2. Student carries ProgramID and ApplicantID as direct FKs. This is a
   deliberate denormalization justified by query frequency: every academic
   workflow starts with the student's program or identity without needing to
   traverse Application. The Application record is still preserved as the
   admissions audit trail; Student does NOT replace it.
3. Course is owned by a School (Course.SchoolID), NOT a Program.
   Program<->Course is a real M:N captured in ProgramCourse with a boolean
   IsCore and a recommended Semester (1..10).
4. Enrollment separates Grade (nullable ENUM) from AttendancePercentage.
   There is no sentinel-value overloading on the Grade column.
5. An Application's outcome is captured in Application.Status and, upon
   selection, a separate Offer row is issued. Offer carries its own lifecycle
   (IssueDate, ExpiryDate, Status) independent of the application status.
6. Application stores snapshot scores (SnapshotHSScore, SnapshotBestTest,
   AggregateScore) at submission time so that later changes to the Applicant's
   underlying scores do not retroactively alter the admission decision record.
7. Prerequisite is a self-referencing M:N on Course; a course can have many
   prerequisites and be a prerequisite of many others.
8. Classroom is standalone (no SchoolID FK) — rooms are shared across schools
   and are identified by (Building, RoomNumber).
9. ON DELETE CASCADE is used for the junction tables (TestAttempt, Application,
   ProgramCourse, Prerequisite) so deleting a parent sweeps its junction rows.
   Other FKs use the default RESTRICT to protect academic records.
10. Indexes exist on high-traffic FK/filter columns (Enrollment.StudentID,
    Enrollment.SectionID, Section.CourseCode/TermID/FacultyID, Application.Status,
    Application.ProgramID, TestAttempt.TestID, Course.SchoolID,
    ProgramCourse.CourseCode).
11. The schema ships with two views (StudentTranscript, ClassroomUtilization),
    one stored procedure (AcceptAdmission), and one function
    (IsEligibleForEngineering).

TRIGGERS
--------
- EnforceClassCapacity           BEFORE INSERT on Enrollment
    (rejects the row if the hosting Section's Classroom is at capacity)
- AutoUpdateApplicationStatus    AFTER INSERT on Student
    (flips the referenced Application from 'Selected' to 'Enrolled')

GRADE LETTERS
-------------
Allowed values on Enrollment.Grade:
    A, A-, B+, B, B-, C+, C, C-, D+, D, F
Grade NULL means the enrollment is still in progress or was withdrawn.

================================================================================
EXACT CARDINALITIES BETWEEN ENTITIES
================================================================================

ACADEMIC MODULE
---------------
School       1 ---- 1..*  Faculty         (Faculty.SchoolID NOT NULL; a school
                                           must employ at least one faculty member)
School       1 ---- 1..*  Program         (Program.SchoolID NOT NULL)
School       1 ---- 1..*  Course          (Course.SchoolID NOT NULL; courses are
                                           owned by their teaching school)

Program      *  ---- *    Course          (via ProgramCourse; junction adds
                                           IsCore boolean + RecommendedSemester)

Course       1 ---- 0..*  Section         (Section.CourseCode NOT NULL; a course
                                           need not have a section every term)
Course       *  ---- *    Course          (via Prerequisite self-referencing
                                           junction; a course may have none)

Term         1 ---- 1..*  Section         (Section.TermID NOT NULL; every term
                                           must host at least one section)
Faculty      1 ---- 0..*  Section         (Section.FacultyID NOT NULL; a faculty
                                           member need not teach every term)
Classroom    1 ---- 0..*  Section         (Section.ClassroomID NOT NULL)

Student      1 ---- 1..*  Enrollment      (Enrollment.StudentID NOT NULL; a
                                           student must be enrolled in at least
                                           one section)
Section      1 ---- 1..*  Enrollment      (Enrollment.SectionID NOT NULL; a
                                           section must have at least one student)

ADMISSIONS MODULE
-----------------
Applicant    1 ---- 1..*  Application     (Application.ApplicantID NOT NULL;
                                           every applicant must have applied)
Program      1 ---- 1..*  Application     (Application.ProgramID NOT NULL)
Term         1 ---- 1..*  Application     (Application.TermID NOT NULL; applications
                                           are tied to an intake term)
Applicant    1 ---- 1..*  TestAttempt     (TestAttempt.ApplicantID NOT NULL; every
                                           applicant must have at least one attempt)
EntryTest    1 ---- 1..*  TestAttempt     (TestAttempt.TestID NOT NULL)

CROSS-MODULE LINK
-----------------
Applicant    1 ---- 0..1  Student         (Student.ApplicantID NOT NULL and UNIQUE;
                                           an applicant may or may not enroll)
Program      1 ---- 0..*  Student         (Student.ProgramID NOT NULL; a program
                                           can have zero enrolled students initially)
Application  1 ---- 0..1  Offer           (Offer.ApplicationID NOT NULL; most
                                           applications receive no offer)

================================================================================
TABLE INVENTORY
================================================================================
  1. School        (PK SchoolID;        UQ SchoolName)
  2. Faculty       (PK FacultyID;       FK SchoolID; UQ Email)
  3. Program       (PK ProgramID;       FK SchoolID; UQ (SchoolID, ProgramName))
  4. Course        (PK CourseCode;      FK SchoolID)
  5. Prerequisite  (PK (CourseCode, PrereqCourseCode); FK Course x2)
  6. ProgramCourse (PK (ProgramID, CourseCode); FK Program, Course)
  7. Term          (PK TermID;          UQ TermName)
  8. Classroom     (PK ClassroomID;     UQ (Building, RoomNumber))
  9. Student       (PK StudentID;       FK ProgramID, ApplicantID; UQ Email)
 10. Section       (PK SectionID;       FK CourseCode, TermID, ClassroomID, FacultyID)
 11. Enrollment    (PK (StudentID, SectionID); FK Student, Section)
 12. Applicant     (PK ApplicantID;     UQ Email; UQ CNIC)
 13. EntryTest     (PK TestID)
 14. TestAttempt   (PK (ApplicantID, TestID); FK Applicant, EntryTest)
 15. Application   (PK ApplicationID;   FK ApplicantID, ProgramID, TermID;
                    UQ (ApplicantID, ProgramID, TermID))
 16. Offer         (PK OfferID;         FK ApplicationID)

================================================================================
COLUMN CHEAT-SHEET (the most common query-time columns)
================================================================================
School        : SchoolID, SchoolName, Abbreviation, EstablishedYear
Faculty       : FacultyID, SchoolID, FullName, Email, Designation
Program       : ProgramID, SchoolID, ProgramName, DegreeType,
                TotalSemesters, TotalCredits, TotalSeats
Course        : CourseCode, SchoolID, CourseTitle,
                CourseType ('Core'/'Elective'/'Lab'), CreditHours, ContactHours
Prerequisite  : CourseCode, PrereqCourseCode
ProgramCourse : ProgramID, CourseCode, RecommendedSemester, IsCore (BOOLEAN)
Term          : TermID, TermName (ENUM e.g. 'Fall','Spring','Summer'),
                AcademicYear, StartDate, EndDate
Classroom     : ClassroomID, Building, RoomNumber, Capacity
Student       : StudentID, ProgramID, ApplicantID, FullName, Email,
                CurrentSemester, EnrollmentDate
Section       : SectionID, CourseCode, TermID, ClassroomID, FacultyID,
                SectionLabel
Enrollment    : StudentID, SectionID, AttendancePercentage, Grade
                (Grade NULL = in progress or withdrawn)
Applicant     : ApplicantID, FullName, CNIC, Email, HighSchoolBoard (ENUM),
                HighSchoolScore, BestTestScore
EntryTest     : TestID, TestType (ENUM), TestDate, TotalMarks
TestAttempt   : ApplicantID, TestID, Score
Application   : ApplicationID, ApplicantID, ProgramID, TermID,
                SnapshotHSScore, SnapshotBestTest, AggregateScore,
                SubmissionDate,
                Status ('Pending','Selected','Waitlisted','Rejected',
                        'Enrolled','Declined')
Offer         : OfferID, ApplicationID, IssueDate, ExpiryDate,
                Status ('Pending','Accepted','Expired','Declined')

================================================================================
COMMON JOIN RECIPES
================================================================================

Student -> Program / School:
    Student JOIN Program ON Program.ProgramID = Student.ProgramID
            JOIN School  ON School.SchoolID   = Program.SchoolID

Student -> Applicant (name, contact):
    Student JOIN Applicant ON Applicant.ApplicantID = Student.ApplicantID

Student -> Admissions record (application + offer):
    Student JOIN Applicant   ON Applicant.ApplicantID = Student.ApplicantID
            JOIN Application ON Application.ApplicantID = Applicant.ApplicantID
                             AND Application.ProgramID  = Student.ProgramID
            LEFT JOIN Offer  ON Offer.ApplicationID = Application.ApplicationID

Student transcript (course + term + grade):
    Enrollment JOIN Section  ON Section.SectionID  = Enrollment.SectionID
               JOIN Course   ON Course.CourseCode  = Section.CourseCode
               JOIN Term     ON Term.TermID         = Section.TermID

Curriculum for a program:
    ProgramCourse JOIN Course   ON Course.CourseCode  = ProgramCourse.CourseCode
                  JOIN Program  ON Program.ProgramID  = ProgramCourse.ProgramID

Applicant best NET score:
    Applicant LEFT JOIN TestAttempt ON TestAttempt.ApplicantID = Applicant.ApplicantID
    (use MAX(TestAttempt.Score) in a GROUP BY, or read Applicant.BestTestScore)

Course prerequisites:
    Course c1 JOIN Prerequisite p ON p.CourseCode      = c1.CourseCode
              JOIN Course      c2 ON c2.CourseCode      = p.PrereqCourseCode

================================================================================
VIEWS, PROCEDURES, FUNCTIONS (defined in NUST.sql)
================================================================================
  VIEW StudentTranscript       - per-enrollment row with student name, program,
                                 course, term, grade, and attendance
  VIEW ClassroomUtilization    - sections hosted per classroom with capacity

  PROCEDURE AcceptAdmission(p_application_id, p_enrollment_date)
                                - transactionally creates Student row and issues
                                  Offer; the Application is auto-promoted to
                                  'Enrolled' by the trigger

  FUNCTION  IsEligibleForEngineering(p_applicant_id)
                                - TRUE iff applicant's best Engineering-type
                                  NET score is >= 140
